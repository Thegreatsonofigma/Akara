# Akara NDPC Readiness Pack

This folder contains Akara's working data protection and NDPC readiness pack.
It is written for Akara Fintech Solutions, a WhatsApp-first, AI-assisted peer-to-peer currency exchange coordination product.

Akara does not currently hold customer funds, operate a wallet, provide escrow, act as a bank, act as a remittance company, act as a bureau de change, or move money for users. Akara provides software tools that help verified users discover, list, coordinate, evidence, and track peer-to-peer exchange arrangements.

These documents are not legal advice and do not, by themselves, certify Akara with the Nigeria Data Protection Commission. They are implementation-ready drafts that should be reviewed by a Nigerian data protection lawyer or licensed Data Protection Compliance Organisation before submission, audit filing, public publication, or certification claims.

## Certification Readiness Path

1. Confirm the business identity, CAC details, registered address, official support channels, and privacy contact.
2. Complete and approve this policy pack internally.
3. Publish user-facing Privacy Notice, Terms, Cookie Notice, KYC Notice, Dispute Rules, and WhatsApp summaries.
4. Appoint a privacy owner or DPO contact for Akara.
5. Finalise vendor contracts, Data Processing Agreements, and cross-border transfer assessments.
6. Implement retention, deletion, access control, audit logging, breach response, and dispute evidence controls in the product.
7. Conduct a formal compliance audit with a qualified DPCO or Nigerian privacy counsel.
8. Complete any required NDPC registration, audit return, or certification process through the official NDPC route.
9. Keep evidence of compliance decisions, training, audits, incidents, and user rights requests.

## Key Akara Assumptions

- Launch countries: Nigeria, Rwanda, Ghana, Kenya, and Cameroon.
- Launch currencies: NGN, RWF, GHS, KES, and XAF.
- Launch users: verified individuals, including students, expats, travellers, freelancers, community members, and other Africans needing peer-listed exchange options.
- Verification: users may browse basic information, but must verify before creating listings, opening Akara Trades, adding payout accounts, or completing exchanges.
- KYC provider: a third-party KYC provider such as Didit until a final provider is selected.
- Payout rule: Akara does not allow third-party payout accounts at launch. Saved payout names should match the user's verified legal name, with minor spelling differences sent to admin review.
- Launch fee: free during launch. Any future fee must be shown before a trade is opened or completed.
