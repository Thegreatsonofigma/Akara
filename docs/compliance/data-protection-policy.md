# Akara Data Protection Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy explains how Akara protects personal data while operating a WhatsApp-first, AI-assisted peer-to-peer currency exchange coordination service.

Akara helps verified users:

- browse peer-listed exchange offers;
- create rate listings;
- open Akara Trades;
- share listing cards and links;
- add payout details;
- upload receipts and evidence;
- raise disputes;
- receive admin review and account tier decisions.

Akara does not hold funds, provide custody, provide escrow, operate a wallet, move money, remit money, act as a bank, or act as a bureau de change. Users send money directly to each other through their own bank or mobile money providers.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- Akara Fintech Solutions;
- Akara's WhatsApp bot and WhatsApp Flows;
- Akara's admin dashboard;
- Akara's website and support channels;
- Akara's KYC, payout, listing, trade, receipt, dispute, and risk workflows;
- employees, founders, admins, contractors, processors, and third-party vendors who process Akara user data.

## 3. Users Covered

Akara is designed for verified individuals, including:

- students;
- expats;
- travellers;
- freelancers;
- cross-border workers;
- African community members;
- other verified users transacting within supported currency corridors.

Users must be at least 18 years old.

## 4. Supported Markets At Launch

Akara's first legal and operational scope covers:

- Nigeria;
- Rwanda;
- Ghana;
- Kenya;
- Cameroon.

Launch currencies:

- NGN;
- RWF;
- GHS;
- KES;
- XAF.

Akara may expand to additional African markets and currencies after legal, operational, and data protection review.

## 5. Data Protection Principles

Akara will process personal data using the following principles:

- Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency: users should know what Akara collects and why.
- Purpose limitation: data is used only for Akara's product, security, support, compliance, and dispute purposes.
- Data minimisation: Akara collects only what is needed for verification, payout matching, listings, trades, safety, and compliance.
- Accuracy: users can correct profile, verification, and payout details, subject to safety checks.
- Storage limitation: data is kept only for the retention periods defined in this policy and applicable law.
- Security: Akara protects data using technical and organisational controls.
- Accountability: Akara keeps records, policies, audit logs, and evidence of compliance decisions.

## 7. Personal Data Akara Processes

### Identity And Account Data

- full legal name;
- WhatsApp phone number;
- country of nationality;
- country of residence;
- city;
- student or expat context where provided;
- verification status;
- account tier;
- restrictions or penalties.

### KYC And Verification Data

- government-issued ID type;
- ID image or document;
- selfie or liveness image;
- document metadata;
- face match result;
- verification decision;
- admin review notes;
- rejection or appeal status.

Accepted documents may include national ID, passport, driver's licence, residence permit, or other government-issued ID accepted by Akara.

### Payout Data

- bank account name;
- bank name;
- account number;
- mobile money account name;
- mobile money phone number;
- mobile money network;
- supported currency;
- payout verification status;
- name-match result.

Akara does not allow someone else's payout account at launch. Payout names should strictly match the verified legal name. Minor spelling differences may go to admin review.

### Listing And Trade Data

- currency offered;
- currency requested;
- amount offered;
- amount requested;
- rate;
- terms, including negotiable or fixed;
- listing reference;
- trade reference;
- trade status;
- expiry window;
- cancellation and completion status.

### Receipt, Evidence, And Dispute Data

- bank receipt;
- mobile money receipt;
- SMS confirmation;
- app screenshot;
- PDF receipt;
- transaction screenshot;
- dispute description;
- supporting documents;
- admin decision;
- dispute outcome.

Akara may reject evidence that is blurry, cropped, edited, mismatched, suspicious, or inconsistent with the trade.

### Messaging And Support Data

- WhatsApp message content needed to provide the service;
- message sessions;
- user commands and menu selections;
- support messages;
- complaint records;
- marketing opt-in or opt-out status.

### Security And Audit Data

- admin actions;
- audit logs;
- suspicious behaviour flags;
- repeated cancellations;
- duplicate account signals;
- passcode or sensitive-action metadata;
- webhook event metadata;
- IP or browser data where collected on the website or admin dashboard.

## 8. Lawful Bases

Akara processes personal data under the following lawful bases, where applicable:

- Contract or pre-contractual steps: to create accounts, show offers, open trades, manage listings, process payout details, and support users.
- Consent: for optional marketing, optional analytics where required, and specific verification or biometric checks where consent is required.
- Legal obligation: to keep compliance, KYC, fraud, dispute, and audit records where required by law.
- Legitimate interests: to prevent fraud, protect users, investigate disputes, secure the platform, improve product quality, and enforce rules.
- Vital or public interest: only if required in exceptional legal or safety circumstances.

Akara must explain these bases clearly in its user-facing Privacy Notice.

## 9. Verification Rules

Users may browse basic information before verification. Users must be verified before they can:

- create a listing;
- open an Akara Trade;
- add or edit payout details;
- complete exchanges;
- upload receipts as trade evidence;
- receive higher account limits.

Tier guidance:

- Tier 1: low-value trades after basic verification.
- Tier 2: medium limits after stronger checks.
- Tier 3: higher limits after admin-approved KYC.

If the document, selfie, legal name, and payout name match, Akara may approve the user automatically. If there is a mismatch, suspicious record, unclear document, or risky payout change, the user should go to admin review.

## 10. Trade And Safety Rules

Minimum launch trade amounts:

| Currency | Minimum |
| --- | ---: |
| NGN | 1,000 |
| RWF | 1,000 |
| KES | 100 |
| GHS | 10 |
| XAF | 1,000 |

Trade rules:

- trades expire after 15 minutes unless extended by product logic;
- users should not trade outside Akara after opening an Akara Trade;
- users cannot freely cancel after marking payment as sent;
- users must raise a dispute if payment was sent and value was not received;
- fake receipts may lead to restriction, permanent ban, dispute escalation, and possible reporting.

## 11. Fees

Akara is free during the launch period. Fees may apply later and must be shown clearly before a user opens or completes a trade.

## 12. User Rights

Users may request:

- access to their personal data;
- correction of inaccurate data;
- deletion, subject to legal, fraud, dispute, and retention limits;
- restriction or objection to processing;
- portability where technically feasible;
- withdrawal of consent where processing is based on consent;
- review of an automated or admin decision where applicable.

Requests can be made through WhatsApp, `support@tryakara.com`, or `complaints@tryakara.com`.

## 13. Retention

Akara's launch retention rules:

| Data Type | Retention |
| --- | --- |
| KYC records | 5 years after account closure or last transaction, unless law requires longer. |
| Receipts and dispute records | 5 years. |
| WhatsApp chat records | 2 years, unless linked to dispute, fraud, legal, or compliance review. |
| Admin audit logs | 5 years or longer if required for compliance or investigation. |
| Marketing opt-out records | As long as needed to respect the opt-out. |

Deletion requests should be handled within 30 days where possible. Deletion may be paused during active dispute, fraud, legal, or compliance review.

## 14. Cross-Border Transfers

Akara may use vendors that process data outside Nigeria or the user's country. Akara must maintain a vendor register, contracts, Data Processing Agreements, transfer assessments, and security evidence for vendors such as:

- Meta and WhatsApp;
- Supabase;
- Vercel;
- Cloudflare;
- KYC providers such as Didit;
- AI or parsing providers where used;
- email and support providers;
- analytics providers where used.

## 15. Security Controls

Akara should maintain:

- row-level security for database records;
- least-privilege admin access;
- admin audit logs;
- secure webhook validation;
- encrypted storage or restricted bucket access for IDs, selfies, and receipts;
- signed or time-limited URLs for sensitive files;
- payout name matching;
- account tier limits;
- dispute evidence controls;
- duplicate account and self-trading detection;
- passcode or WhatsApp Flow authorisation for sensitive actions;
- backups and recovery checks;
- breach response procedures.

## 16. Restrictions And Enforcement

Akara may restrict, suspend, or ban users for:

- fake receipts;
- payout name mismatch;
- abandoned trades;
- repeated cancellations;
- bypass attempts;
- suspicious high-value activity;
- fraud reports;
- abusive behaviour;
- trading outside Akara after opening a trade.

Confirmed fraud may lead to permanent ban and possible reporting.
