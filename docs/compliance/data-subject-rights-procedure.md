# Akara Data Subject Rights Procedure

## 1. Purpose

This procedure explains how Akara handles user requests about personal data.

Users may request access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, portability, consent withdrawal, or review of certain decisions.

## 2. Request Channels

Users may contact Akara through:

- WhatsApp;
- `support@tryakara.com`;
- `complaints@tryakara.com`.

## 3. Intake

For every request, Akara should record:

- request date;
- user's WhatsApp number;
- user's legal name where available;
- request type;
- active listings, trades, disputes, or restrictions;
- assigned reviewer;
- due date;
- outcome;
- response date.

## 4. Identity Check

Before releasing or changing personal data, Akara must confirm the requester is the account owner.

Acceptable checks may include:

- WhatsApp number match;
- verified legal name;
- recent KYC status;
- passcode or WhatsApp Flow authorisation;
- selfie or admin review for sensitive requests.

## 5. Active Dispute Or Fraud Hold

Akara may pause deletion or restriction where the account is linked to:

- active trade;
- active dispute;
- fraud review;
- fake receipt investigation;
- legal request;
- compliance audit;
- suspected abuse.

The user should be told that the request is on hold and why, without disclosing sensitive internal risk signals.

## 6. Response Target

Akara should aim to respond within 30 days.

If a request is complex, Akara should notify the user, explain the delay, and keep an internal record.

## 7. Request Types

### Access

Provide a readable copy or summary of:

- account profile;
- verification status;
- payout details on file;
- live and historical listings;
- trade history;
- dispute history;
- receipts where release is safe;
- account restrictions.

Do not expose another user's private information unless required for a trade, dispute, legal process, or the user already had access during the trade.

### Correction

Allow correction of inaccurate profile data, subject to:

- payout name matching;
- verification review;
- passcode checks for sensitive fields;
- admin review for risky changes.

### Deletion

Delete or anonymise data where legally and operationally possible.

Do not delete data needed for:

- active trades;
- disputes;
- fraud reviews;
- audit logs;
- compliance records;
- legal obligations;
- receipts and KYC retention.

### Restriction Or Objection

Stop or limit processing where appropriate, unless Akara has a lawful reason to continue.

### Portability

Provide structured export where technically feasible.

### Consent Withdrawal

Withdraw optional marketing or optional consent-based processing. This does not affect required security, trade, dispute, KYC, or compliance messages.

## 8. Refusal Or Partial Fulfilment

Akara may refuse or partially fulfil a request where:

- identity cannot be verified;
- request would expose another user's data;
- request conflicts with dispute, fraud, legal, or compliance duties;
- request is abusive, repetitive, or unsafe;
- retention is legally required.

The reason should be recorded and explained to the user in clear language.

## 9. Escalation

Escalate to the privacy lead or legal reviewer when:

- the user threatens legal action;
- the request involves KYC deletion;
- the request involves a dispute or fraud allegation;
- the request involves a regulator;
- the request involves cross-border transfer concerns.

## 10. Evidence

Keep a rights request log for 5 years, including:

- request;
- verification method;
- decision;
- response;
- data exported or deleted;
- reason for refusal or hold, if any.
